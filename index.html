About

I am an economist at the Research Department of Danmarks Nationalbank and a research affiliate at IZA. My research interests are applied Macroeconomics and Labor Economics. Before joining Danmarks Nationalbank, I did my Ph.D. studies at the University of Zurich. You can find an overview of my research projects here, and my CV here.

In my Job Market Paper "The Pass-Through of Minimum Wages into US Retail Prices: Evidence from Supermarket Scanner Data", I study the response of grocery stores' prices to increases in minimum wages. In other ongoing projects, I analyze exchange rate pass-through and manufacturing employment dynamics after the Swiss franc shock of 2015, the relationship between labor cost and automation in the US retail industry, and the response of retail prices to demand shocks during the great recession.
